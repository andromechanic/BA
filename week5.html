01

The Optimization Paradigm

Optimization in business analytics is the mathematical process of maximizing or minimizing a target metric (like revenue or cost) subject to a set of realistic restrictions. We call the metric the Objective Function, and the limitations are the Constraints.

The Core Business Metrics

  • Revenue: The gross income generated ($P \times Q$). It maps as a downward-facing parabola against linear demand.

  • Profit ($\pi$): Revenue minus total operational costs. Profit isolates the margin: $(P - c) \times Q$, where $c$ is the marginal cost to manufacture one good.

02

Calculus of Maximization

Assuming a linear demand response curve $Q(P) = D_0 - mP$, we can derive the optimal price ($P^*$) using the First Order Condition (setting the first derivative to zero).

R

Revenue Maximization

Rev = $P \cdot (D_0 - mP)$

$$ P^* = \frac{D_0}{2m} $$

Calculated by setting $\frac{d(Rev)}{dP} = D_0 - 2mP = 0$.

$\pi$

Profit Maximization

$\pi$ = $(P - c) \cdot (D_0 - mP)$

$$ P^* = \frac{D_0 + mc}{2m} $$

Profit peak will always require a higher optimal price than the revenue peak.

03

LPP Standard Form Rules

Before converting a Linear Programming Problem (LPP) from Primal to Dual, it must be in Standard Form. If a constraint uses the wrong inequality sign, you must multiply the entire constraint row by $-1$ to flip the sign.

Objective Required Constraint Sign Fixing Equality ($=$)
Maximization $\le$ or $<$ Split into $\le$ and $\ge$, then multiply the $\ge$ equation by $-1$.
Minimization $\ge$ or $>$ Split into $\ge$ and $\le$, then multiply the $\le$ equation by $-1$.
04

Primal-Dual Conversion Algorithm

The Dual is a mirrored perspective of the Primal problem. The conversion mechanism transposes coefficients, flips objectives, and swaps structural boundaries.

Original Primal

  • Objective: Maximize $\rightarrow$
  • Number of Constraints ($m$) $\rightarrow$
  • Number of Variables ($n$) $\rightarrow$
  • Objective Coefficients ($C_j$) $\rightarrow$
  • Constraint RHS Limits ($B_i$) $\rightarrow$

Resulting Dual

  • $\rightarrow$ Objective: Minimization
  • $\rightarrow$ Number of Dual Variables ($Y_m$)
  • $\rightarrow$ Number of Dual Constraints
  • $\rightarrow$ Dual Constraint RHS Limits
  • $\rightarrow$ Dual Objective Coefficients
05

Binding vs. Non-Binding Constraints

Once optimal decision variables ($X^*$) are found, plugging them back into the original constraints reveals whether a resource was completely exhausted (Binding) or if there is leftover slack (Non-Binding).

Crucial Limit

Binding Constraint

When optimal values are substituted, the Left Hand Side perfectly equals the Right Hand Side ($LHS = RHS$). The resource is fully consumed.

Dual Variable Implication:

Non-Zero (Strictly > 0)

Slack Exists

Non-Binding Constraint

When optimal values are substituted, an inequality remains ($LHS \neq RHS$). There is slack or surplus resource available.

Dual Variable Implication:

Might be Zero
