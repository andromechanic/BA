01

Mathematical Translation (LPP)

Before solving, you must correctly map business restrictions into mathematical constraints. Your Decision Variables are the explicit levers you control (e.g., how much to invest in $X_1, X_2...$).

The Investment Portfolio Scenario

  • Q: "Bongu Oil ($X_2$) cannot be more than 60% of the total Oil Industry investment ($X_1 + X_2$)."
  • A: $X_2 \le 0.60 \cdot (X_1 + X_2)$
02

Standard Form & Equality Rules

In a standard Maximization Primal problem, all constraints must be $\le$. Strict equalities (e.g., "Must invest exactly ₹1,00,000") break this rule and must be fractured into two valid inequalities.

Constraint Splitting

Original Equality

$$ \sum X_i = 100,000 $$

Part 1 (Naturally Valid)

$\sum X_i \le 100,000$

Part 2 (Sign Flipped)

$-\sum X_i \le -100,000$

Originally $\ge$, multiplied by $-1$ to force the valid $\le$ format.

03

Non-Linear Calculus (The Formula Trap)

If the demand curve is not linear, you cannot blindly use the formulas $P^* = D_0/2m$ or $(D_0+mc)/2m$. You must revert to fundamental calculus derivatives (First Order Conditions).

$\frac{d}{dP}$

Given Problem

Demand: $D(P) = 1444 - 3P^2$
Cost: $c = 50$

1. Revenue Maximization

$Rev = P \cdot (1444 - 3P^2) = 1444P - 3P^3$

Set $Rev' = 0 \implies 1444 - 9P^2 = 0$

$P^* = 12.67$

2. Profit Maximization

$\pi = (P - 50) \cdot (1444 - 3P^2)$

Set $\pi' = 0 \implies \text{Quadratic Equation}$

$P^* = 37.60$

⚠️ Business Trap: While mathematically valid, $P=37.60$ forces $D(P)$ to become negative. In reality, demand cannot be negative!

05

Evaluating Binding Constraints

To determine which Dual Decision Variables equal zero, you must test the Primal optimal solution ($X^*$) against the original Primal constraints.

No Slack Left

Binding Constraint

Test: $13(X_1) + 26(X_2) \le 1000$

Result: $1000 \le 1000$

The Left Hand Side perfectly equals the Right Hand Side ($LHS = RHS$). The resource limit is fully reached.

Dual Variable Property:

Non-Zero Value

Slack Exists

Non-Binding Constraint

Test: $45(X_1) + 10(X_2) \le 5000$

Result: $3800 \le 5000$

An inequality remains ($LHS \neq RHS$). You have not used up the maximum allowance for this specific constraint.

Dual Variable Property:

Strictly Zero
